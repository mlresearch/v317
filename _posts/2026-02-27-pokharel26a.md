title: From Policy to Logic for Efficient and Interpretable Coverage Assessment
abstract: Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated strong capabilities in interpreting
  lengthy, complex legal and policy language. However, their reliability can be undermined
  by hallucinations and inconsistencies, particularly when analyzing subjective and
  nuanced documents. These challenges are especially critical in medical coverage
  policy review, where human experts must be able to rely on accurate information.
  In this paper, we present an approach designed to support human reviewers by making
  policy interpretation more efficient and interpretable. We introduce a methodology
  that pairs a coverage-aware retriever with symbolic rule-based reasoning to surface
  relevant policy language, organize it into explicit facts and rules, and generate
  auditable rationales. This hybrid system minimizes the number of LLM inferences
  required which reduces overall model cost. Notably, our approach achieves a 44%
  reduction in inference cost alongside a 4.5% improvement in F1 score, demonstrating
  both efficiency and effectiveness.
